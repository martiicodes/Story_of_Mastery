1. Align with Life Purpose: Ignite Your Work with Meaning

"Life is never made unbearable by circumstances, but only by lack of meaning and purpose."*
— Man's Search for Meaning by Viktor E. Frankl

Having a higher purpose gives your work meaning and keeps you engaged, especially during inevitable challenging times. Finding your WHY is crucial. Your "why" is engine that drives you. Reflect on what truly matters to you. What's your impact on the planet Earth? Perhaps, on Mars?

Once you identify your why , it becomes your compass. Simon Sinek's book Start with Why: How Great Leaders Inspire Everyone to Take Action is a great launching pad!

How to Do It with Design:

- Visual Identity: or Visual Brand DNA. Ignite your potential. Use classic design techniques to express your values and mission. This unites people. They're inspired by your vision. All visionaries do this!

Good design is consistent design.

Massimo Vignelli was master of timeless, minimalist design. He proved that simplicity and clarity are powerful. Across all disciplines.

He said that "Consistency in design translates to reliability and trust in the brand."

Consistency is the key for a brand's development!. The key for a brand's prestige is positioning .

Now, let me mention a great branding book. Our company uses this book's insights in our projects all the time. This vault of game-changing ideas is Positioning: The Battle for Your Mind . Written by Al Ries and Jack Trout.

Get an unfair advantage with golden ideas from the book:

"Effective positioning requires a clear and consistent message that reinforces the brand’s unique identity."
"The best way to succeed in marketing is to be the first in the minds of consumers."
"The idea of positioning is to differentiate your brand in a way that stands out and resonates."

On top of that: Pay close attention to this gems:

"To win the battle for the mind, you must focus on one key idea that sets your brand apart." "Positioning is a way to create a mental image of a brand that influences consumer behavior."

- Visual Tale: Use eye-catching graphics. Tell the story. What's your mission? Values?

This is your visual saga and you're the creator. How fun is that?

Keep in mind that every art and design choice has a reason. Example: black implies sophistication and authority. It's used in luxury and high-end products.

We tell stories all the time. Through our clothes, looks and actions. We're already multimedia storytelling experts. Want proof: our carefully curated social media profiles.

What if we leverage this power? Consciously harness it?

We can become architects of our lives.

4. Reframe Challenges: Unlock the Power of a Growth Mindset

As you read this, you may begin to see fresh new possibilities. Maybe new doors of opportunities open up to you and now you feel refreshed with new pleasant feelings. But, hey! Wait until you hear this!

Typically, people experience the world through fixed mindset. They think they aren't able to become better. That their circumstances are somehow fixed.

In contrast to that, growth mindset helps you see challenges as opportunities to learn and improve. Challenges help you grow! A growth mindset is the belief that you can improve and get better through effort and learning.

How to Do It with Design:

- Visual Problem-Solving: Break down challenges into micro-components. Visualize them. Make them more manageable. What you can see you can measure. What you can measure you can manage.

Find optimal point of challenge.

- Images of Growth: Imagine bettering yourself. Find or create representations of your potential.

Make image of your ideal future self. And strive to be closer to it daily. Attractive is not good enough. It should energize you, moves yo. Idea of it, becoming a reality, makes you extatic!
